# 7-Eleven — Competitor Screenshot Analysis

## Overview
7-Eleven's mobile app focuses on **loyalty/rewards**, wallet, and subscription perks. The screenshot shows the **Home screen** with a rewards tracker, membership perks, and promotional banners.

---

## Color Usage
- **Primary palette**: Clean white background with bold accent colors — the design is deliberately minimal and brand-driven.
- **Brand green**: Deep 7-Eleven green (`~#006241`) used in the bottom promotional banner ("$10 OFF $10+") — strong brand recognition.
- **Brand yellow/gold**: Bright yellow (`~#FFD700`) used for the "Gold Pass™ Perks" section background — signals premium/VIP status.
- **Brand red/orange**: Used in the 7-Eleven logo stripes and accent elements.
- **Progress bar colors**: Multi-tone green gradient in the rewards progress bar — darker filled portion, lighter unfilled.
- **Black text on white**: High contrast, clean readability throughout.
- **Minimal color bleeding**: Each section has its own color story (white for rewards, yellow for perks, green for promo) — clear visual segmentation.

## Typography
- **Greeting**: Extra-bold, large black sans-serif ("Hi [name]!") — warm, personalized.
- **Membership badge**: Small caps, gray text ("MEMBER SINCE 2025") with a small icon — understated loyalty signal.
- **7REWARDS branding**: Custom bold, colored wordmark in brand red/green/orange.
- **Points display**: Large, bold "300 pts" in black — high visual weight for the reward balance.
- **Progress text**: Small, gray informational text ("Only 200 points away to unlock new rewards").
- **CTA buttons**: Bold, dark text in rounded pill buttons with dark borders ("View Deals").
- **Perks section**: Mixed bold headings ("7 FREE Drinks per Month") with standard body text — clear value propositions.

## Layout & Spacing
- **Single-column vertical stack**: Very clean, card-based layout scrolling vertically. No horizontal scrolling patterns.
- **Greeting header**: Simple left-aligned greeting with location and profile icons on the right. Minimal header — no search bar on home.
- **Rewards card**: Full-width card with internal structure: logo + points → progress bar → milestone labels → wallet balance. Clear information hierarchy.
- **Perks section**: Yellow background card containing a 2×2 grid of perk cards (3 visible). Each perk card has an illustration + bold text.
- **Promotional banner**: Full-width, full-bleed banner at the bottom with a different color scheme (green) and a clear "Claim Now" CTA.
- **Bottom nav**: 5-tab navigation (Home, Order, Deals & Rewards, Fuel, Scan, Pay, Go) — longer text labels than usual.
- **Chat/support FAB**: Small dark floating action button in the bottom-right corner of the promo banner.

## Design Patterns
- **Personalization**: Greeting by name + membership tenure creates a personal, loyal relationship feel.
- **Progress/gamification**: Rewards progress bar with milestones (0 → 500 → 5000+) — classic gamification pattern showing progress toward next reward.
- **Wallet integration**: "7-Eleven Wallet $1.00" shown inline with rewards — connects spending with earning.
- **Subscription perks grid**: Clean 2×2 layout showcasing Gold Pass benefits with simple illustrations — scannable at a glance.
- **Value-first content**: Every section communicates clear monetary value ("FREE Drinks", "5¢/gal Off Fuel", "$10 OFF").
- **Bordered card design**: Yellow perks section uses a subtle dashed/solid border around the card — creates visual containment without heavy shadows.
- **First-order incentive**: Bottom banner targets new/inactive users with first-order discount — re-engagement pattern.

## Spacing & Sizing Observations
- **Greeting zone**: ~80px including name and membership badge.
- **Rewards card**: ~160px tall with comfortable internal padding (~16px).
- **Progress bar**: ~8px height, spanning full card width with milestone markers.
- **Perk cards**: ~150px × 120px each in the 2×2 grid with ~12px gap.
- **Corner radii**: ~16px on main cards, ~8px on internal perk cards.
- **Overall vertical spacing**: ~16-24px between major sections — very breathable layout.
- **Bottom nav**: Standard ~50px height.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Loyalty/rewards-first design** — the entire home screen is organized around earning and spending rewards, not browsing products.
2. **Extremely clean and minimal** — lots of whitespace, simple card layout, no visual clutter. Stark contrast to Gopuff and Swiggy.
3. **Gamification through progress** — the rewards progress bar is a textbook example of motivating continued engagement.
4. **Warm personalization** — greeting by name and showing membership tenure builds emotional connection.
5. **Color segmentation** — white, yellow, and green sections create clear visual zones without needing complex layouts.
6. **Subscription upsell** (Gold Pass) is prominently placed but feels like a benefit showcase, not a hard sell.
7. **Minimal navigation** — no search, no category browsing on home. The app prioritizes loyalty engagement over product discovery.
